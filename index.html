Agile/Scrum Team Workflow

Created by Emily Porta for Shift Health

What is agile? What is scrum?

Agile: project management term for short bursts of work, frequent iterations/adaptations. Deliver bits all the time, not all at once. Working software over everything.

The general philosophy for project management in software development.

What is agile? What is scrum? (cont.)

Scrum: a specific set of rules to conduct agile-type development work.

Self-organizing, largely independent teams working on feature stories prioritized by the "product owner"

Key scrum terms

  • Roles: Product Owner, Team, Scrum Master
  • Events: Sprint/iteration, sprint ceremonies
  • Artifacts: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, (Potentially Shippable) Product Increment

Roles

Product Owner: Dan

  • Primary purpose is to prioritze the sprint backlog and communicate market/business situation/needs/strategy to the Team
  • The "CEO" of the product, and the voice of the stakeholders/clients

Roles (cont.)

Scrum Master: Emily

  • Facilitates the scrum team getting things done - NOT a project manager
  • Helps team out with any scrum-related information needed, keeps team on task, removes all impediments to getting development work done
  • Helps product owner manage product backlog, facilitates all team events/scrum ceremonies

Roles (cont.)

Team: Le, Roumen, Emily + everyone else!

  • Usually just the developers plus Dan/Sandy, but can be whoever needs to be brought into this team to get the feature done

Events!

In this (and cyclical) order:

  • Sprint Planning
  • Daily Scrum/Standup
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retrospective

Also: Backlog refinement (meeting)

Artifacts

  • Product Backlog
  • Sprint Backlog
  • Potentially Shippable Product Increment

Product Backlog

Made up of Product Backlog Items (stories (bugs, chores, features), epics, tasks) that will be prioritized and eventually pulled in in order into the Sprint Backlog

Constantly changing, being updated, evolving, in a just-in-time method in a process called "backlog refinement"

Sprint Backlog

The backlog items for the upcoming sprint

PSPI

Potentially Shippable Product Increment: the goal of the sprint

Pivotal and Shift

We use pivotal tracker to organize all of this

Benefits: calculating and tracking velocity and volatility, basically like an electronic white board with sticky notes on beast mode

Points help us estimate timelines and mark when we might have PSPI ready for testing
